1. Introduction

Welcome to Tutelio ("we," "our," or "us"). This Cookie Policy explains how we use cookies and similar tracking technologies on our AI-powered educational platform ("Platform").

By accessing or using Tutelio, you agree to the use of cookies as described in this policy. This Cookie Policy should be read alongside our Privacy Policy and Terms and Conditions.

2. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to the website owners. Cookies help enhance user experience by:

  • Remembering your preferences and settings
  • Ensuring secure areas of the website function properly
  • Collecting information about how you use the website
  • Enabling personalized content and features

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the Platform to function properly and cannot be switched off in our systems. They are usually set in response to actions made by you, such as setting your privacy preferences, logging in, or filling in forms. You can set your browser to block these cookies, but some parts of the Platform may not work properly.

Examples of essential cookies we use:

  • Authentication cookies: To identify you when you log in to our Platform
  • Security cookies: To support security features and detect malicious activity
  • Session cookies: To remember information as you navigate through the Platform

3.2 Preference Cookies

These cookies enable the Platform to remember choices you make and provide enhanced, personalized features. They may be set by us or by third-party providers whose services we have added to our pages.

Examples of preference cookies we use:

  • Language cookies: To remember your preferred language
  • Customization cookies: To remember your display preferences
  • Font size cookies: To remember your preferred text size

3.3 Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our Platform by collecting and reporting information anonymously. They help us improve the way our Platform works and measure the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns.

Examples of analytics cookies we use:

  • Performance cookies: To count visits and traffic sources
  • Usage pattern cookies: To understand how users navigate through the Platform
  • Error logging cookies: To help us improve by measuring any errors that occur

3.4 Functionality Cookies

These cookies allow the Platform to provide enhanced functionality and personalization, such as remembering your learning progress, preferences, and settings.

  • Progress tracking cookies: To remember your learning progress
  • User interface customization cookies: To remember specific user interface settings
  • Interactive feature cookies: To enable interactive elements of the Platform

3.5 Educational AI Cookies

These specialized cookies help our AI-powered educational tools understand your learning patterns and provide personalized educational experiences.

  • Learning style cookies: To remember your learning preferences
  • Progress pattern cookies: To analyze and adapt to your learning pace
  • AI interaction cookies: To improve AI responses based on your interactions

4. Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies are placed by third parties on our behalf. These third parties may include analytics providers, content delivery networks, and educational technology partners. These third-party services are carefully selected and may place cookies on your device when you visit our Platform.

Third-party cookies may include:

  • Analytics providers: To help us understand how users engage with our Platform
  • Educational content providers: To deliver and optimize educational material
  • Media players: To enable multimedia content on our Platform
  • Social media plugins: To allow content sharing and integration with social platforms

Please note that we do not control third-party cookies. We recommend reviewing the privacy and cookie policies of these third parties for more information.

5. Cookie Duration

Cookies on our Platform can be classified by their duration:

  • Session Cookies: These are temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser. They enable the Platform to recognize you as you navigate between pages.
  • Persistent Cookies: These remain on your device for a specified period or until you delete them manually. They help us recognize you as a returning visitor and remember your preferences for future visits.

6. Managing Cookies

6.1 Cookie Consent

When you first visit our Platform, you will be presented with a cookie banner that allows you to accept or decline non-essential cookies. You can change your preferences at any time through our Cookie Preferences Center accessible from the footer of our Platform.

6.2 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to manage your cookie preferences. You can:

  • Delete cookies from your device
  • Block cookies by activating settings on your browser
  • Choose to block all cookies, or only third-party cookies
  • Clear cookies automatically when you close your browser

Please note that restricting cookies may impact your experience of our Platform and limit the functionality we can provide.

6.3 How to Manage Cookies in Popular Browsers

For information on how to manage cookies in your browser, please visit:

  • Google Chrome: Chrome Cookie Settings
  • Microsoft Edge: Edge Privacy Settings
  • Mozilla Firefox: Firefox Cookie Settings
  • Apple Safari: Safari Cookie Settings

7. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers incorporate a Do Not Track (DNT) feature that signals to websites you visit that you do not want to have your online activity tracked. Our Platform does not currently respond to DNT signals. However, you can usually choose to set your browser to remove or reject cookies.

8. Cookies and Children

Our Platform is designed for users of all ages, including children under 13. For users identified as under 13 years of age:

  • We only use cookies that are necessary for Platform functionality and educational purposes
  • We limit data collection to what is reasonably necessary
  • We obtain verifiable parental consent before setting non-essential cookies
  • Parents/guardians can review and request deletion of their child's cookie data

9. Updates to This Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in technology, regulation, or our business practices. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last Updated" date. We encourage you to review this Cookie Policy periodically to stay informed about our use of cookies.

If we make significant changes, we will provide a more prominent notice, such as an email notification or a temporary banner on our Platform.
